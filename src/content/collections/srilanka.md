Sri Lanka welcomed me slowly, the way the island itself moves — without hurry. After the noise of bigger journeys, here I found a different rhythm: the green of the tea fields, the salt of the ocean, the patience of people who smile before they speak.

What I felt most was gentleness. A country that doesn't try to impress you, but quietly stays with you — in the warm light of early morning, in a passing glance, in the sense that, for once, there was nowhere else I needed to be.
